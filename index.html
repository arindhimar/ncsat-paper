Abstract

This research paper presents a deep learning-based approach for generating high-quality Marathi Text-to-Speech (TTS) using an end-to-end architecture. Marathi, being a morphologically rich Indo-Aryan language, poses challenges due to limited annotated datasets, dialectal variations, and complex prosody. The proposed system utilizes a VITS-based architecture integrating text normalization, phoneme conversion, and prosody-aware acoustic modeling. The model is trained on a multi-speaker Marathi speech corpus to enhance naturalness and intelligibility.

The study compares the proposed system with traditional concatenative methods and neural baselines such as Tacotron2. Evaluation is performed using objective metrics like Mel-Cepstral Distortion (MCD), F0 Root Mean Square Error (RMSE), and Character Error Rate (CER), along with subjective Mean Opinion Score (MOS). Results demonstrate significant improvements in naturalness, clarity, and prosody modeling. This work contributes toward scalable TTS systems for low-resource Indian languages and has applications in audiobooks, accessibility tools, virtual assistants, and e-learning platforms.

Keywords

Marathi TTS, Deep Learning, VITS, AI Speech Synthesis, NLP, Prosody Modeling
